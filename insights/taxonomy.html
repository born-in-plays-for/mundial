Every way a country gets classified here, on one diagram

Nested rings for the strict hierarchy — every country the map lists at all is either FIFA or not; every FIFA member sits in exactly one of six confederations; the 48 qualified teams are scattered across those confederations, not confined to one. A fine hatch marks which countries are on the map at all. Export and import get their own diagram further down — they're not a confederation-shaped question, so they don't live on this wheel.

Deliberately not called "Elo-rated" — two of the countries listed here (Kosovo, a FIFA/UEFA member; Isle of Man, non-FIFA) have never actually been given a rating by eloratings.net. They're listed anyway, with a placeholder instead of a real score, purely so they can still appear as ordinary countries — a different situation from the 6-country "oddities" pocket below, which do have real ratings, just no official country code.

This is deliberately an Euler diagram, not a Venn diagram: regions are sized and nested to match which combinations actually occur (qualified ⊆ FIFA ⊆ has-confederation), not drawn as equal independent circles. A true flat Venn across all six dimensions would draw regions — "qualified, non-FIFA" — that are structurally impossible.

Export / import — a different lens on the same 239

This has nothing to do with confederation — it's a split of where a country sits relative to qualification, so it gets its own diagram instead of sitting on top of one wedge. Left: inside the 48 qualified teams, three concentric groups. Right: the other 191, where only "exports or not" applies (no squad to import into).

FIFA disc → confederation wedges. Six wedges, angularly sized by real member count (213 total across the six sets, vs. 211 FIFA members — a couple of geography-only outliers double-count harmlessly). Non-FIFA countries sit outside the disc entirely — no confederation.
Qualified — 48. Drawn as a band cutting through every wedge rather than nested in one, because that's what actually happens: qualified teams are FIFA members first, and only incidentally grouped by confederation.
Exports — 51. Any country with a player who left, qualified or not — 32 also import, 19 don't have a squad to import into at all. See the separate diagram below, not tied to any wedge.
Imports — 47. Only possible for a qualified team — 32 also export, 15 import only, and exactly one qualified team (Czech Republic) does neither at all.
On the map. Fine hatch across almost everything; the two punched-through circles (Monaco, Singapore) are the rare off-map exceptions — both non-FIFA.
Oddities — 6. Kurdistan, Zanzibar, Tibet and 3 others: real Elo ratings, but reported under a made-up code with no official country-code equivalent — so no wedge of their own, just a dashed pocket floating in the non-FIFA band. (Northern Cyprus and Somaliland used to live here too, until each was given a code of its own — now ordinary non-FIFA pills.)
The uncolored majority is the point. 172 of the 239 countries here — 72% — sit outside every colored band: non-qualified, and with zero players in this dataset. That's not a gap in the diagram; it's the actual shape of global football talent flow. The 67 countries touched by qualification, export, or import are the whole visible story — everything else is quiet by default, not by omission.
